## Recipe details

- **Cuisine:** chinese
- **Difficulty:** easy
- **Total time:** 30 min
- **Servings:** 4
- **Tags:** rice, quick, vegetarian

## Ingredients

- 3 tbsp neutral oil (canola or grapeseed)
- 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
- 1 red bell pepper, roughly chopped
- 2 carrots, finely minced
- 1 cup peas (defrost if frozen)
- 1 tbsp garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp ginger, minced
- 3–4 cups cooked white rice, cooled
- 2 eggs, lightly beaten
- ¼ cup Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry, or water)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- Salt and black pepper
- ½ cup cilantro or scallions, minced

<!-- GENERATED_RECIPE_METADATA_END -->

## Steps

1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add onion, pepper, and carrots. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and starting to brown, 5–10 minutes. Remove to a bowl with a slotted spoon.
2. Add peas to the skillet and cook for about a minute until hot. Add to the bowl.
3. Add remaining 2 tbsp oil to the skillet. Add garlic and ginger and cook until fragrant, about 15 seconds.
4. Add rice, breaking up clumps and tossing to coat with oil.
5. Make a well in the centre of the rice, pour in the eggs and scramble them, then stir into the rice.
6. Return vegetables to the skillet and stir to combine.
7. Add Shaoxing wine and cook, stirring, for about 1 minute.
8. Add soy sauce and sesame oil. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
9. Turn off heat, stir in cilantro or scallions and serve.

## Notes

- Cold day-old rice is key — fresh rice turns mushy. Make a big batch of basmati ahead and refrigerate.
- Dry sherry works as a Shaoxing wine substitute.
- A small drizzle of fish sauce while cooking adds depth (takeout-style flavour).
