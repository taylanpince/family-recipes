ingredients:
  - 4 lbs russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, cut into large chunks
  - 2 quarts water
  - 1 oz (≈ 2 tbsp) salt
  - ½ tsp baking soda
  - 5 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  - 3 cloves garlic, minced or pressed
  - Handful of fresh rosemary, chopped
  - Salt and fresh cracked pepper
  - Fresh parsley, chopped (to finish)
---

## Steps

1. Bring 2 quarts of water to a boil. Add salt and baking soda.
2. Add potatoes and boil ~10 minutes until a knife pierces easily and the exteriors are starting to fall apart.
3. While potatoes boil, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat with rosemary and garlic. Cook until garlic just turns golden, then strain immediately into a large bowl. Reserve the garlic and rosemary.
4. Drain potatoes, let steam for a minute, then add to the bowl with infused oil. Season with salt and pepper. Toss roughly — you want a pasty, mashed-potato-like coating to build up on the exterior of each chunk.
5. Preheat oven to 450°F (400°F convection). Spread potatoes on a rimmed baking sheet with space between pieces.
6. Roast 20 minutes without touching, then flip and continue roasting 30–40 minutes more, flipping occasionally, until deeply brown and crisp all over.
7. Transfer to a bowl, add reserved garlic, rosemary, and fresh parsley. Toss to coat and serve immediately.

## Notes

- The baking soda makes the water alkaline, breaking down pectin on the potato surface → craggier texture → more crunch.
- Boiling in already-boiling (not cold) water concentrates the effect on the outer edges.
- Be rough when tossing in the oil — the rougher the coating, the crispier the result.
