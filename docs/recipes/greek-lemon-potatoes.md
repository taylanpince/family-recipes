## Recipe details

- **Cuisine:** greek
- **Difficulty:** easy
- **Total time:** 90 min
- **Servings:** 6
- **Tags:** potatoes, sides, greek, vegan, gluten-free

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs (900g) Yukon Gold potatoes, cut into 1-inch wedges
- 4 cloves garlic, coarsely chopped
- ½ cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
- ⅓ cup olive oil (Greek if possible)
- ¼ cup yellow mustard
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- Fresh ground black pepper
- ½ cup water

<!-- GENERATED_RECIPE_METADATA_END -->

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Grease a 9×13-inch baking dish.
2. Whisk together garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, mustard, oregano, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
3. Toss potato wedges in the mixture to coat well.
4. Pour into the baking dish in an even layer. Pour water over the top.
5. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 1 hour until fork-tender.
6. Uncover and bake a further 15–20 minutes until liquid evaporates and potatoes start to brown.

## Notes

- The secret ingredient is yellow mustard — don't substitute with Dijon, it changes the flavour.
- Can be held at room temperature for a few hours and reheated for 10 minutes before serving.
- Leftovers are excellent in omelettes or eaten cold.
