## Recipe details

- **Difficulty:** easy
- **Total time:** 50 min
- **Servings:** 4
- **Tags:** seafood, clams, quick

## Ingredients

- 4 tbsp unsalted butter
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small shallot, finely chopped
- 4–5 cloves garlic, minced
- Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1 cup dry white wine (Pinot Grigio, Sauvignon Blanc, or Vinho Verde)
- 1–1.5 kg fresh littleneck or Manila clams, cleaned
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Fresh Italian parsley, finely chopped
- Crusty bread to serve

<!-- GENERATED_RECIPE_METADATA_END -->

## Steps

1. 30 minutes before cooking, soak clams in a large bowl of cold salted water to purge sand. Scrub shells with a brush. Discard any that won't close.
2. Heat butter and olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat until butter is nearly melted.
3. Add shallot and cook 2–3 minutes until just softened.
4. Add garlic and red pepper flakes, cook 1–2 minutes more.
5. Pour in white wine and let reduce for 1–2 minutes.
6. Add clams, stir, and cover with a lid — leave a small gap for steam to escape.
7. Cook 7–9 minutes until all clams open. Discard any that don't open.
8. Squeeze lemon juice over the top. Garnish with parsley and serve immediately with crusty bread.

## Notes

- Use unsalted butter — clams are naturally salty.
- Have extra crusty bread ready, the broth is the best part.
- Clams should be cooked the same day they're purchased.
