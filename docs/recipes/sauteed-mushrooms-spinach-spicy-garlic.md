ingredients:
  - 4 cups sliced mushrooms (shiitake or portobello)
  - 4 cups loosely packed baby spinach
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 2 tsp sesame seeds
  - 1 tsp minced garlic
  - ¼ cup scallions, chopped
  - 3 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  - 1 tbsp sesame oil
  - 1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  - ½ tsp sugar
---

## Steps

1. Toast sesame seeds in a dry pan over medium-low heat until golden, moving frequently. Transfer to a cutting board and crush with a heavy knife.
2. Whisk together crushed sesame seeds, garlic, scallions, soy sauce, sesame oil, red pepper flakes, and sugar. Set aside.
3. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mushrooms and cook until golden, 5–7 minutes.
4. Add spinach, then pour the sauce over everything.
5. Cook for another 5 minutes, then serve.

## Notes

- Works as a side with chicken, pork, or shrimp, or as a meat-free main.
- Sauce is the star — the Korean-inspired spicy/savory balance transforms ordinary veg.
