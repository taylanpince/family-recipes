## Recipe details

- **Cuisine:** greek
- **Difficulty:** medium
- **Total time:** 135 min
- **Servings:** 6
- **Tags:** lamb, creuset, braised, greek

## Ingredients

- 6 bone-in lamb loin chops
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- ¼ cup flour (or arrowroot starch for gluten-free)
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 cup chicken broth
- ½ cup red wine
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard

<!-- GENERATED_RECIPE_METADATA_END -->

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 150°C (300°F).
2. Season lamb generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. Mix flour with a pinch of salt and cayenne. Dredge lamb in the flour to lightly coat.
4. Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear lamb on both sides until browned, about 3 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
5. Add onion and smashed garlic to the pot. Sauté until onions soften, about 4 minutes.
6. Add chicken broth, red wine, mustard, oregano, and bay leaf. Stir and scrape up any brown bits.
7. Nestle the lamb back into the sauce.
8. Cover and cook in the oven at 150°C for 2 hours.
9. Serve over mashed potatoes with a Greek salad.

## Notes

- The Dijon goes in the braising liquid — don't skip it, it adds great depth.
- Arrowroot starch works as a Whole30/GF substitute for the flour dredge.
