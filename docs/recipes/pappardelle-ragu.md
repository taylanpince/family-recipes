## Recipe details

- **Cuisine:** italian
- **Difficulty:** medium
- **Total time:** 180 min
- **Servings:** 4
- **Tags:** pasta, beef, creuset, ragu

## Ingredients

- Beef (braising cut — chuck or short rib work well)
- Olive oil
- Salt
- 4–5 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 carrots, chopped
- 2 stalks celery, chopped
- 1 cup red wine
- 2 cans whole or crushed tomatoes
- 1.5–2 cups meat stock (enough to cover)
- Parmesan, shredded (to finish)
- Pappardelle pasta

<!-- GENERATED_RECIPE_METADATA_END -->

## Steps

1. Pat beef dry and season generously with salt.
2. Heat olive oil in the Creuset over medium heat. Sear beef on all sides until well browned, about 2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
3. Add a splash more olive oil, turn heat to medium-low. Add garlic and onion, sauté 2 minutes.
4. Add carrots and celery, sauté 5–7 minutes until tender.
5. Add red wine and simmer for a few minutes.
6. Add canned tomatoes and simmer a few minutes more.
7. Return beef to the pot and add meat stock — liquid should just cover the meat.
8. Cook covered for 2–2.5 hours (or longer) until beef falls apart easily with a fork.
9. Remove lid and cook a bit longer to reduce and thicken the sauce.
10. Shred beef into the sauce. When reheating, stir in shredded Parmesan until melted and combined.
11. Serve over cooked pappardelle.

## Notes

- Better the next day — make ahead if possible.
- Chuck or bone-in short rib give the best flavour and texture.
